# Everything You Need to Know About Entry-Level Data Scientist Salaries

Heard that data scientists make six figures right out of school? They *can*, but expectations need to be realistic. Entry-level data scientist pay varies a lot depending on location, industry, and company size. Some new grads earn $165,000 at tech companies in San Francisco, while others start closer to $79,000 in smaller markets.

Also important: base salary isn’t the whole picture. Bonuses, stock options, and negotiation can significantly increase total compensation.

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects a **34% increase in data scientist jobs by 2034**, and you don’t need a technical bachelor’s degree to break into the field.

---

## What Is the Average Salary for an Entry-Level Data Scientist?

According to Glassdoor, entry-level data scientists in the US earn:
- **Base salary:** $79,000 – $131,000
- **Additional pay (bonuses, equity):** $24,000 – $44,000

Your earnings depend on:
- Skill set
- Location
- Industry
- Portfolio quality
- Employment type (full-time vs contractor)

## Bootcamp vs Bachelor’s Degree for Data Science

Glassdoor doesn’t differentiate salaries between bootcamp grads and degree holders. Because demand is high, **bootcamp grads can earn comparable entry-level salaries**.

Why bootcamps work:
- Faster completion (often under 1 year)
- Lower cost than college
- Strong job placement support

## Skills Entry-Level Data Scientists Need

### Core Technical Skills
- Python (critical)
- SQL (non-negotiable)
- R (helpful)
- Statistics & probability
- Machine learning basics (regression, classification)
- Pandas, NumPy
- Data visualization (Tableau, Power BI)

### Soft Skills That Matter
- Explaining insights to non-technical stakeholders
- Exploratory data analysis
- Clear communication

## Career Growth & Salary Progression

- First raise: typically **7–10% within 12–18 months**
- Entry-level average: ~$88,797 total comp
- After 1+ year: ~$101,055 (+14%)
- Mid-level (5 years): $204,000 – $220,000 total

Biggest salary jumps usually come from switching companies.

## FAQ

### Can You Get a Data Science Job Without a Degree?
Yes. About **25% of data scientists** entered through non-traditional paths like bootcamps. Employers care most about:
- Projects
- Python & SQL skills
- Applied ML experience

### Does Remote Work Affect Salary?
Remote entry-level roles typically pay **$70,000 – $95,000**, sometimes matching on-site salaries in high-cost regions.

---

### What Does Total Compensation Include?
- Bonuses: 5–15%
- Equity / RSUs: 10–25%
- Sign-on bonuses: $5,000 – $15,000
- RSUs can add $5,000 – $12,500 per year

---

### How Much Can You Negotiate?
Most candidates can negotiate **10–20% higher** compensation. If base salary is fixed, negotiate:
- Bonuses
- Equity
- Remote flexibility
- Professional development budget

---

### How Fast Does Salary Grow?
- 12–18 months: +7–10%
- Job switching every 2–3 years: +15–25% each move

Entry-level salaries are just the beginning.
